# Business Case

## Core Thesis

`After Exit` works only if it is built as a `high-ARPU media-plus-community company`, not as a traffic business.

The opportunity is attractive because the audience is:

- economically dense
- identity-rich
- under-served by current founder and investor media
- useful to premium partners
- likely to pay for curation, access, and privacy

## Willingness To Pay

The audience already pays for adjacent products at meaningful price points.

### Media anchors

- `The Information`: `$399/year`, or `$749/year` for Pro
- `Every`: paid bundle with content, products, community, and events

### Community anchors

- `TIGER 21`: `$34,000/year` North America dues
- `TIGER 21 Family Office Groups`: `$51,500/year`

### Lifestyle anchors

- `Soho House`: recurring membership model at large scale
- `Summit`: curated event and retreat culture as product

## Recommended Price Ladder

### 1. Reader

`$300` to `$600/year`

This is not a mass subscription. It should include digital access, issue packages, early event access, and select community touchpoints.

### 2. Circle

`$2,500` to `$7,500/year`

For founders who want private salons, member directory access, curated introductions, and private notes.

### 3. Patron

`$12,000` to `$25,000/year`

For top-tier access: retreats, host privileges, private briefings, premium partner routing, and family-office or philanthropy programming.

### 4. Founding partners

`$40,000` to `$150,000+` annually per partner, depending on category rights, event access, custom briefings, and content studio work.

## Meaningful Differentiation

This concept is differentiated against other magazines on at least five fronts.

### 1. Next-chapter principal lens

Most business magazines focus on builders before exit or operators still in the grind. This publication starts where those publications stop and follows the move into investing, stewardship, family leadership, and second-act design.

### 2. Full-life coverage with credible business depth

The mix of entrepreneurship, wealth, family, wellness, spirituality, travel, leisure, reputation, and self-presentation is unusual. Most media brands split these into different products. The reader experiences them as one life.

### 3. PEF adjacency

PEF gives the project a real trust wedge with exactly the right audience. That is more defensible than starting from cold-start distribution.

### 4. Embedded documentary storytelling

One of the strongest original story avenues is to embed inside:

- a founder's new startup
- a family office build-out
- a founder household
- a training or recovery regimen
- a philanthropic project
- a travel pattern or retreat

This creates a point of difference from text-first business media and from glossy but shallow luxury magazines.

### 5. Media-plus-community economics

This does not have to win by scale. It wins by trust density, partner quality, and high-value extensions.

## Why It Can Work

Even a small, highly qualified audience can support the business.

If the brand reaches only a tiny percentage of the U.S. and global post-exit founder market, it can still support:

- premium memberships
- intimate events
- high-end sponsors
- referral marketplaces
- partner intelligence products
- documentary editorial franchises

The business case improves if the publication becomes the place where accomplished founders go to understand what comes after winning once.
