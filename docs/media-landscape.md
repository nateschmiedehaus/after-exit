# Media Landscape

## The Moment

The broad media market is difficult. The niche premium media market is still viable.

That distinction matters.

### 1. General-interest digital news has a payment ceiling

The Reuters Institute's `Digital News Report 2025` says that across 20 richer countries, only `18%` of people pay for online news. That is useful because it shows why broad news subscriptions are hard to scale now.

Source: [Digital News Report 2025](https://sanef.org.za/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Digital_News-Report_2025.pdf)

### 2. Commodity volume is losing to distinctiveness

On `March 11, 2025`, Axios reported Washington Post executive editor Matt Murray saying the Post was writing too much commodity news and should write "less in total, but more in impact."

Source: [Axios Media Trends, March 11 2025](https://www.axios.com/newsletters/axios-media-trends-6a3f5cd0-fdda-11ef-9d47-6d4ca95699f8)

### 3. Creator-led and niche paid media continue to grow

Axios reported on `March 11, 2025` that Substack had `more than 5 million paid subscribers`. The same Axios report said `over 40%` of Substack subscribers pay for publications that publish video, and `82%` of Substack's 250 highest-revenue creators use audio and video.

Source: [Axios Media Trends, March 11 2025](https://www.axios.com/newsletters/axios-media-trends-6a3f5cd0-fdda-11ef-9d47-6d4ca95699f8)

### 4. The winning media products are bundles, not just articles

`Every` says its plans include:

- full article access
- products
- Discord community
- events and exclusive access

`The Information` sells:

- `$399/year` standard
- `$749/year` Pro
- proprietary databases
- org charts
- networking tools

Sources:

- [Every bundle details](https://help.every.to/en/articles/13225758-every-bundle-special-programs)
- [The Information subscription page](https://www.theinformation.com/subscribe_h)

### 5. Community brands are already monetizing affluent identity

`TIGER 21` describes itself as an exclusive peer network for wealth creators and says it has `1,700+ members` across `59 cities`. Its 2026 pricing sheet lists North America dues at `$34,000` and Family Office Groups at `$51,500`.

`YPO` says it has `38,000+ members` across `150+ countries`.

`Soho House` reported `271,541` total members at year-end 2024, including `212,447` Soho House members and a waitlist of `111,417`.

Sources:

- [TIGER 21 about](https://tiger21.com/about-us/)
- [TIGER 21 pricing PDF](https://tiger21.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/2026-Membership-Pricing_Nov25.pdf)
- [YPO reach](https://www.ypo.org/acf/)
- [Soho House 2024 results](https://www.sohohouseco.com/news-and-events/news-details/2025/Soho-House--Co-Inc--Announces-Fourth-Quarter-and-Fiscal-Year-2024-Results/default.aspx)

### 6. Media brands are expanding into strange but lucrative side products

The Free Press runs `FP Cupid`, a paid-subscriber matchmaking product. This matters less because matchmaking is the right move here, and more because it proves that strong media brands can extend into identity- and community-based services that would look absurd in a commodity publication.

Source: [FP Cupid](https://www.thefp.com/p/fp-cupid)

## What This Means

The right comparison set for this project is not:

- Condé Nast-style scale media
- general startup news
- mass-luxury lifestyle magazines

The right comparison set is a hybrid of:

- niche paid media
- executive communities
- members' clubs
- events businesses
- partner-backed intelligence products

## Competitive Set

### Closest adjacent media brands

- `The Information`: premium, narrow, high-utility, but not lifestyle or post-exit.
- `Every`: media plus tools, courses, events, and community, but aimed at builders broadly rather than wealthy post-exit founders.
- `Air Mail`, `Monocle`, `Kinfolk`: high taste and strong visuals, but not post-exit founder-specific.

### Closest adjacent communities

- `PEF`: exact audience trust wedge, but not a standalone media brand.
- `TIGER 21`: strongest proof that post-liquidity peers will pay for confidentiality and structure.
- `YPO`: proof that executive community can scale globally.
- `Summit`: proof that curated event culture can become the product.
- `Soho House`: proof that recurring membership plus hospitality can support a lifestyle identity layer.

## Whitespace

No major publication currently combines all of the following:

1. a post-exit founder lens
2. credible editorial treatment of business and capital
3. serious coverage of body, family, spirituality, and reputation
4. PEF-adjacent trust and distribution
5. documentary-style access into founders' companies, homes, training routines, travel, and second-act projects

That final point is important. The strongest original story avenue here is not only essays. It is `embedded stories`: photo essays and short films inside startups, family offices, households, private rooms, and the actual lived environments of ambitious people after exit.
