# Editorial Roadmap

## Editorial Position

`After Exit` is for founders who have already won one obvious game and are now trying to win the harder one: building a life that deserves the exit.

## Issue Franchises

1. `The Meaning Issue`  
   Essays on purpose, drift, depression after achievement, spiritual practice, and the psychology of enough.
2. `The Body Issue`  
   Training, diagnostics, supplements, recovery, sleep architecture, and the economics of feeling powerful at 45, 55, and 65.
3. `The Family Issue`  
   Founder marriages, parenting after liquidity, household operating systems, and protecting children from distorted incentives.
4. `The Capital Allocation Issue`  
   How exited founders think about liquidity, portfolio design, private markets, philanthropy, and the risk of becoming over-financialized.
5. `The Reputation Issue`  
   Self-promotion, media training, speechwriting, online visibility, narrative control, and when to stay quiet.
6. `The Travel Issue`  
   Intentional travel, work-leisure blending, host standards, memberships, clubs, and destination intelligence for high-agency people.
7. `The Home Issue`  
   Primary homes, second homes, club ecosystems, interior design, hospitality rituals, and what domestic life signals about values.
8. `The Next Venture Issue`  
   Search funds, acquisition entrepreneurship, micro-PE, angel strategy, advisory roles, and operating again without becoming trapped.
9. `The City Guide Issue`  
   Founder-friendly guides to New York, Austin, Miami, London, Zurich, Lisbon, and other nodes where capital, status, and culture mix.
10. `The Legacy Issue`  
   Heirs, education, family constitutions, governance, giving, and multigenerational coherence.

## Advertiser Fit

Best categories:

- private banks and wealth platforms
- premium travel and hospitality brands
- wellness diagnostics and longevity providers
- home, design, and property services
- executive education and advisory firms
- selective B2B brands that sell directly to founder-operators

Worst categories:

- generic performance marketers
- low-trust crypto promotions
- mass-market wellness products
- spray-and-pray startup sponsorships
