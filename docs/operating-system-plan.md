# Operating System Plan

## Principle

Build `After Exit` like a high-trust editorial company with community and commercial extensions, not like a media site with a few events bolted on.

Editorial is the trust engine.
Rooms are the conversion engine.
Partners are the monetization layer.
Operations exist to protect quality and cadence.

## 1. Editorial Organization

### Core team at launch

- `Editor in Chief`
  Owns story standards, issue architecture, contributor direction, and editorial worldview.

- `Managing Editor`
  Owns calendar, workflow, copy flow, deadlines, and packaging.

- `Visual Director`
  Owns photography, art direction, image sourcing, issue feel, and video taste.

- `Publisher / Commercial Lead`
  Owns partnerships, sponsor packaging, and revenue planning.

- `Community and Events Lead`
  Owns dinners, salons, member journeys, host operations, and room quality.

### Specialist bench

- contributing editors
- freelance reporters
- fact-checker
- copy editor
- photographers in key cities
- freelance film/video editors

## 2. Contributor Model

Use a mixed model:

- marquee contributors for signal
- operator-practitioners for specificity
- private conversations adapted into publishable frameworks
- embedded reporting inside companies, homes, and family-office builds

Contributor lanes:

- essays
- reported features
- annotated interviews
- photo essays
- short films
- private member briefings

## 3. Visual, Photo, and Video Model

### Photography

Lead with documentary-style, real-environment photography:

- dinners
- homes
- studios
- training spaces
- travel movement
- offices
- founder tables

Avoid:

- conference-stage clichés
- handshake photography
- fake luxury gloss

### Video

Use video selectively:

- one manifesto film
- room proof from salons and retreats
- embedded founder documentaries
- marquee profiles

Do not build a high-volume video machine at launch.

## 4. Partnerships and Sales

### Commercial structure

- 4 to 8 founding partners max in year one
- no programmatic ad logic
- no cluttered sponsor inventory
- sell category ownership, briefings, and room access

### Sales motion

- founder-led first 10 deals
- direct outbound to hand-built target accounts
- private partner brief rather than generic media kit
- quarterly partner-review cadence

## 5. Membership

### Public layer

- flagship site
- newsletter
- selected films and essays

### Reader

- archive
- issue drops
- early event access

### Circle

- salons
- private notes
- introductions
- directory

### Patron / Council

- retreats
- small circles
- family-governance and stewardship programming

## 6. Community

The community should be layered and invitation-sensitive.

Core formats:

- city dinners
- breakfast salons
- walking conversations
- retreat weekends
- host-led home gatherings
- topic circles

Avoid:

- noisy general chat
- open-forum social products
- scale-first chapter proliferation

## 7. Events

### Year 1 event stack

- 3 to 5 city dinners
- 1 larger salon format
- 1 retreat pilot

### Event operating rules

- guest list is editorial
- partner presence must feel additive
- event recap generates photos, clips, and ideas
- no event without a postmortem

## 8. Tech Stack

### Launch stack

- static site for launch surface
- newsletter platform
- CRM for reader/member/partner pipeline
- Luma for gated events
- Circle for membership and community
- Stripe for billing

### Phase 2 stack

- CMS
- contributor workflow tooling
- member auth
- asset library for photos/video
- analytics dashboard

## 9. Analytics and Decisioning

### Primary metrics

- qualified subscriber growth
- reader-to-member conversion
- sponsor close rate
- sponsor renewal rate
- event attendance and repeat attendance
- reply quality from target readers
- member referral rate

### Content metrics

- completion quality
- saves and forwards
- qualified inbound replies
- partner relevance, not just traffic

## 10. Legal and Compliance

Must-have guardrails:

- clear sponsor labeling
- no undisclosed affiliate arrangements
- no medical or financial claims without source discipline and disclaimers
- media release / image consent for events and embedded stories
- contributor contracts and usage rights
- privacy rules for member data

## 11. Budget Priorities

Spend first on:

1. editorial quality
2. visual quality
3. room quality
4. direct sales capability

Do not overspend early on:

- custom software
- full-time video staff
- elaborate summit production
- broad paid audience acquisition

## 12. Launch Phases

### Phase 1: Claim

- lock thesis
- launch visual site
- publish category, market, and business case
- commission first issue package

### Phase 2: Prove

- publish 3 to 5 flagship pieces
- host first salon
- close first founding partner
- open Reader and Circle waitlists

### Phase 3: Convert

- launch paid layers
- build recurring room cadence
- add one embedded film or photo essay
- create repeatable partner briefings

### Phase 4: Scale Carefully

- expand city presence
- add retreat
- add sector packs
- add family-office and successor programming

## 13. Hiring Sequence

1. editor in chief
2. publisher / commercial lead
3. managing editor
4. community and events lead
5. visual director
6. operations / member success support

Keep specialists freelance until demand is clear.

## 14. KPI Cadences

### Weekly

- editorial pipeline
- top reader signals
- partner pipeline

### Monthly

- revenue
- member conversion
- event quality
- sponsor health

### Quarterly

- issue performance
- segment mix
- pricing review
- partner mix review
- whether the category framing still feels precise

## 15. Operating Rhythm

### Monday

- editorial standup

### Wednesday

- commercial and partner pipeline review

### Friday

- audience and member signal review

### Monthly

- room-review meeting
- calendar reset

### Quarterly

- strategic review across editorial, membership, events, and sponsors

## Bottom Line

The operating system should make one thing true:

Every issue, room, sponsor, and member interaction should strengthen the sense that `After Exit` is the place where next-chapter principals learn how to design the life that follows the first win.
