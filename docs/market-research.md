# After Exit Market Research

Researched on April 10, 2026.

## Core Audience Truth

PEF describes itself as a community for post-exit founders helping each other find "purpose, fulfillment, and meaning" after selling a company. That is the clearest strategic anchor for this publication: the audience is not merely wealthy or ambitious; it is specifically in transition.  
Source: [PEF](https://pef.xyz/)

Yale School of Management's 2025 post-exit entrepreneur research makes the opportunity sharper. In its survey work, post-exit founders report enjoying freedom, open schedules, time with family and friends, and recreational pursuits, but they also report feeling lost, lacking meaning, and missing the feeling of leading a team. Yale also notes that many participants own multiple homes and confront new questions around spending, relocation, and identity. That means the media opportunity is not just "business content for rich founders." It is guidance, taste, and social signaling for people rebuilding a life architecture.  
Source: [Yale SOM PDF](https://som.yale.edu/sites/default/files/2025-04/Exploring%20Six%20Key%20Decisions%20Post-Exit%20Entrepreneurs%20Will%20Have%20to%20Make.pdf)

## Why Now

UBS's `Global Entrepreneur Report 2026` says it surveyed 215 entrepreneur clients and highlights three relevant signals: entrepreneurs are looking for growth in new places, AI remains the biggest technology opportunity, and many are approaching major transition choices tied to forthcoming exits. In other words, the founder audience is still commercially active, still intellectually hungry, and increasingly aware that wealth transition changes the operating system of life.  
Source: [UBS Global Entrepreneur Report 2026](https://www.ubs.com/br/en/wealthmanagement/who-we-serve/entrepreneurs/global-entrepreneur-report.html)

McKinsey's 2025 Future of Wellness research shows US wellness spend at more than `$500B` annually, with `84%` of US consumers saying wellness is a top or important priority. For a founder audience with unusually high discretionary spend and performance pressure, wellness is not a side category. It is a core editorial lane spanning fitness, sleep, diagnostics, recovery, nutrition, and healthy aging.  
Source: [McKinsey Future of Wellness Trends 2025](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/consumer-packaged-goods/our-insights/future-of-wellness-trends)

American Express Travel's 2026 travel trends report shows `40%` of global respondents plan to spend more on travel in 2026 than in the prior year, and `66%` expect to take a trip centered on someone else's milestone. The audience is planning intentional travel around family, celebration, and leisure. Travel therefore belongs in the core product, not as filler lifestyle content.  
Source: [American Express Travel 2026 Global Travel Trends](https://www.americanexpress.com/en-gb/travel/discover/get-inspired/global-travel-trends)

## Benchmark Models

Monocle is the strongest reference for what a premium cross-category media brand can become. Its official `About Us` page describes a system that combines magazine publishing, newsletters, radio, events, books, shops, cafes, and brand collaborations. It also explicitly produces entrepreneurship-focused special issues and live events. The takeaway is structural: the publication should not think of itself as a website with articles. It should think of itself as a taste-making operating system with multiple high-margin surfaces.  
Source: [Monocle About Us](https://monocle.com/about-us/), [The Entrepreneurs Live 2026](https://monocle.com/events/the-entrepreneurs-live-2026-shanghai/)

Every is a useful digital benchmark from the opposite direction. Its FAQ positions the company as media plus software, with subscription value extending beyond articles into products, community, events, and training. That reinforces the case for a bundled founder product rather than a pure publication.  
Source: [Every FAQ](https://every.to/faq), [Introducing Every](https://every.to/news/introducing-every-b6ef22ee-37f0-4965-b220-3ed3f810c156)

## Editorial Implications

The publication should cover seven durable lanes:

1. Capital: liquidity strategy, wealth design, investing, tax, family office basics.
2. Body: fitness, diagnostics, sleep, nutrition, recovery, healthy aging.
3. Spirit: identity, faith, meaning, therapy, grief, reinvention, boredom.
4. Family: marriage, parenting, heirs, boundaries, eldercare, legacy.
5. Taste: travel, hospitality, homes, design, food, leisure, collecting.
6. Signal: reputation, networking, self-presentation, media strategy, philanthropy.
7. Next Build: acquisition, angel investing, operating roles, new ventures, creative projects.

## Commercial Implications

The audience is attractive because it is concentrated, affluent, and professionally influential. The business should therefore avoid low-end traffic monetization and instead build:

- a premium membership
- strategic sponsor packages
- destination events and salons
- executive research briefs
- limited-run commercial partnerships with brands already selling to founders

## Recommendation

Build `After Exit` as a multi-format publication first, a member product second, and an event business third. The editorial voice should be intimate, worldly, and practical. The PEF relationship should give distribution and trust, but the publication still needs enough editorial independence to become a real brand rather than a house newsletter.
