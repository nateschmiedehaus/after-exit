# Operating System

## Core View

The company should run as four coordinated engines:

1. `Editorial`
2. `Membership and community`
3. `Commercial and partnerships`
4. `Production and operations`

If any one of these becomes weak, the system breaks:

- editorial without rooms becomes inert
- rooms without editorial become networking theater
- sponsors without standards destroy trust
- production without discipline makes the whole thing feel amateur

## Cadence

### Weekly

- 1 flagship piece
- 1 utility piece or memo
- 1 founder note / recommendation block
- 1 commercial or member touchpoint

### Monthly

- 1 salon
- 1 sponsor review
- 1 KPI review
- 1 franchise planning meeting

### Quarterly

- 1 issue package
- 1 member or partner survey
- 1 category reset on what is and is not working

## Community System

### Principles

- low-volume
- high-trust
- invitation-aware
- city-based
- host-led

### Core room types

- breakfasts
- dinners
- walking salons
- founder circles
- retreats
- family-office or successor cohorts

### Community workflow

1. identify target attendees
2. curate invitations
3. host the room
4. capture insights, photos, and clips
5. publish recap or convert to private note
6. route strongest members into higher-trust layers

## Commercial System

## Sales motion

1. define sponsor categories
2. build partner deck and calendar
3. sell annual category rights
4. attach salons and intelligence products
5. renew from proof, not promises

## Commercial assets

- annual sponsorships
- salon sponsorships
- intelligence briefings
- content studio packages
- retreat partnerships

## Rules

- no sponsor approval over editorial
- no low-trust categories
- no spray-and-pray ad sales
- no selling data

## Production System

### Workflow

1. pitch
2. commission
3. research and reporting
4. edit
5. design / visual packaging
6. publish
7. distribute
8. repurpose into notes, clips, or partner assets

### Asset capture

Every major room or story should aim to generate:

- article
- photo set
- short clip or trailer
- member note
- sponsor proof, if relevant

## Go-To-Market Sequence

### Stage 0: Positioning

- lock category
- lock brand
- lock partner story with PEF

### Stage 1: Proof

- publish launch site
- publish 3 flagship stories
- produce 1 embedded-life pilot
- close 1 to 2 founding partners

### Stage 2: Rooms

- launch first salon series
- recruit charter members
- build city-based social proof

### Stage 3: Membership

- launch paid Reader tier
- launch Circle cohort
- add private directory and intro workflow

### Stage 4: Expansion

- retreats
- partner intelligence products
- city guides
- reputation and concierge extensions

### Commercial

- partner close rate

### Brand quality

- inbound founder applications
- inbound partner quality
- referral rate from members
- waitlist quality, not just size

## What To Avoid

- launching too many cities too early
- overbuilding software
- generic social content
- conference-scale ambition before room quality is proven
- making the site prettier than the product

## Full Plan Summary

The right operating system is:

- narrow category
- high editorial quality
- image-led publishing
- intimate rooms
- selective sponsors
- layered memberships
- tight production loops

That is the simplest version of a full plan that can actually compound.
