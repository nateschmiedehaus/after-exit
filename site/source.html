After Exit
Economics Operating System Appendix

Operating System

Company design, cadence, and control loops.

After Exit runs as a quarterly magazine venture with a weekly signal layer, a city-based room system, controlled physical distribution, and a narrow commercial stack. Editorial calendar, member calendar, partner calendar, and print calendar sit on the same operating spine.

Company Architecture

Editorial, rooms, revenue, and production share one calendar.

Every issue acts as the planning unit. The issue theme sets story commissions, room programming, partner packaging, print specs, and launch sequence. No unit operates as a side business.

Editorial

Issue slate and canon

Theme selection, commissioning, embedded reporting, editing, visual direction, and archive discipline.

Membership

Rooms and member routing

Applications, charter intake, invitations, host management, member notes, and referral handling.

Commercial

Category rights and partner programs

Issue packages, event rights, account targeting, renewals, benefit integrations, and measurement.

Production

Object and distribution chain

Design, photo operations, print buying, fulfillment, mailing files, venue drops, and circulation reporting.

Team Shape

Small core, specialist bench, explicit owners.

The core team carries judgment, relationships, and calendar control. Specialist work expands and contracts by issue, city, and production load.

Lead 01

Editor in chief / publisher

Owns editorial thesis, issue slate, final approvals, flagship partner fit, and cross-function decisions.

Lead 02

Managing editor / program operator

Owns deadlines, contributor flow, contracts, fact checks, legal review, budget pacing, and issue close.

Lead 03

Membership and city lead

Owns host councils, salons, member communications, charter pipeline, guest approvals, and follow-up.

Lead 04

Founding partner lead

Owns prospect list, category map, package scope, commercial timelines, renewals, and sponsor reporting.

Issue bench

Creative specialists by assignment

Photographers, stylists, filmmakers, copy editors, designers, illustrators, retouchers, and production coordinators work per issue or per story.

Room bench

City operators by event cluster

Hosts, concierges, venue leads, and event labor attach to city moments rather than sitting on permanent headcount.

Issue Cadence

Quarterly print cycle, weekly editorial pulse.

The quarterly issue is the anchor object. Weekly publishing keeps authority warm between issues and creates a steady surface for members and partners.

Weeks 1-2

Commission

Lock theme, core package, lead features, photography access, contributor list, and partner inventory.

Weeks 3-6

Report

Run embedded reporting, founder interviews, visual shoots, research checks, and partner brief collection.

Weeks 7-9

Edit

Shape the issue order, close text, finish fact checks, clear sensitivities, and lock art direction.

Weeks 10-12

Produce

Finalize layouts, proofs, print order, mailing files, digital rollout, and issue launch materials.

Weekly

Extend

Publish letters, clips, member notes, and selected film or audio pieces tied to the current issue.

Room Cadence

Issue launch rooms, city salons, closed circles.

Rooms follow the magazine. Public signal enters through the issue. Trust formation happens in smaller formats with tighter invitation controls.

Quarterly

Flagship launch room

One dinner or salon attached to the new issue, lead story, and highest-priority partner or host cluster.

Monthly

City salon rhythm

Small hosted gatherings in core cities used for sourcing, member conversion, partner introductions, and audience density.

Selective

Closed operating circles

Allocation, succession, health, family-governance, or second-act sessions capped to preserve candor and usefulness.

Room inputs

Host list, guest mix, issue tie-in, follow-up brief

Every room closes with referral notes, editorial leads, member prospects, and partner-account follow-through.

Room controls

Invitation rules, no broad RSVP pools, no loose sponsorship

Guest quality, conversation quality, and post-room yield matter more than attendance volume.

GTM Mechanics

PEF wedge, host expansion, adjacent network capture, public signal.

The first layer comes from existing density. The second layer comes from hosts and editorial subjects. The third layer comes from adjacent principal networks that share the same life-stage problems.

01

PEF seeding

Co-branded notes, founder referrals, member intros, and charter invitations populate the first issues, rooms, and partner list.

02

Host-led growth

City hosts and featured principals each bring concentrated local networks, better guest lists, and warmer partner access.

03

Adjacent expansion

Operator-investors, successors, family-office builders, and owner-principals enter through rooms, referrals, and issue relevance.

04

Public proof

Flagship features, image systems, clips, and issue launches provide visible artifacts that circulate beyond the private rooms.

Acquisition tracking

Source every subscriber, member, partner, and host

PEF, host referral, editorial subject, event invitation, public inbound, and partner introduction stay separated in reporting.

City rollout

Story plus room enters before broad promotion

A city opens with one host cluster, one editorial angle, one partner path, and one controlled gathering sequence.

Commercial Mechanics

Low ad load, narrow categories, integrated packages.

Commercial programs map to issue themes, rooms, and controlled distribution. Page count alone does not define the package.

Reader revenue

Subscription ladder

Reader, Circle, and Patron tiers align to access depth, room access, gifting, and introductions rather than raw content volume.

Partner revenue

Issue and room programs

Packages combine issue presence, room rights, member benefits, controlled placement, and account-specific follow-up.

Control

Scarcity at the page and category level

Single-category ownership, restrained inventory, and editorial adjacency rules keep the object from reading like luxury clutter.

Priority categories

Capital, governance, health, travel, education

These categories fit the decisions principals already make and support integrated programming without tone drift.

Commercial reporting

Account list, room attendance, distribution split, renewal path

Every program closes with concrete delivery evidence rather than vague brand-language recap.

Print Workflow

Editorial close, prepress, print, channel split, circulation close.

Print, mailing, venue placement, and sponsor reporting operate as one chain. Counts remain separated by channel from the first issue forward.

Step 01

Freeze issue scope

Page count, ad positions, inserts, stock, binding, partner versions, and mailing assumptions lock before final files move.

Step 02

Run prepress

Proofs, color checks, trim review, mailing-data hygiene, and vendor approvals close before release to press.

Step 03

Print and bind

Quarterly issue runs through a vendor that handles quality control, fulfillment coordination, and periodical logistics.

Step 04

Split distribution

Paid delivery, gifts, event copies, partner-office copies, and venue stacks route through separate lists and separate counts.

Step 05

Close circulation

Paid, requested, controlled, and event distribution totals roll into internal review and sponsor-facing delivery reports.

Metrics

Track quality of audience, rooms, and renewals.

Dashboard design centers on density, conversion, renewal, and circulation quality rather than cheap reach.

Audience mix

40%+

Subscriber and member share arriving through referral, host, PEF, or subject-led channels.

Room conversion

25%+

Qualified reader-to-room or invited-subscriber-to-room conversion across city salons and launch dinners.

Ad load

< 15%

Share of flagship issue pages allocated to paid commercial inventory.

Distribution split

4+

Paid, gifted, event, controlled-placement, and partner-office channels tracked independently.

Member renewal

70%+

Renewal target for first cohorts, paired with coded churn reasons and host-level retention patterns.

Partner expansion

60%+

Share of partners that renew, increase spend, or move from issue package into room or benefits programs.

First-Year Operating Sequence

Prototype issue, charter cohort, city rooms, second issue, renewal cycle.

The first year builds rhythm before scale. Each quarter closes with an object, a room sequence, a member cohort update, and a partner renewal read.

Quarter 1

Build Issue 01

Assemble the core team, lock the first theme, commission the first feature set, line up initial partners, and open charter intake.

Quarter 2

Launch Issue 01

Mail the first issue, host launch rooms in initial cities, capture member conversions, and close delivery reporting for partners.

Quarter 3

Run Issue 02

Expand host network, tighten invitation controls, refine the member ladder, and deepen adjacent audience entry points.

Quarter 4

Renew and widen

Renew founding partners, review first member cohorts, stabilize city cadence, and prepare the next annual issue slate.

Year close

Reset the operating plan

Reprice where needed, add or cut cities, re-rank partner categories, adjust issue mix, and lock the next-year calendar.
