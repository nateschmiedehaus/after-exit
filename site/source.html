After Exit
Business Case Model Sources

Model

Media, community, rooms, and extensions.

The company should be built like a selective editorial platform for next-chapter principals, with memberships, salons, partner products, and embedded-life storytelling. Editorial creates trust. Community and commercial layers deepen value.

Revenue

Start with high-trust lines

Founding partnerships, memberships, and city salons first. Retreats, courses, and marketplaces later.

Supply Chain

Lean studio, outsourced specialists

Keep editorial leadership internal. Outsource photo, video editing, event labor, and concierge fulfillment until volume justifies staff.

Community Layers

Public, member-only, and invite-only.

The right community model is not a noisy forum. It is a set of increasingly private rooms where founders, investors, successors, and stewards can actually learn from each other.

Public

Magazine and newsletter

Flagship essays, photo essays, films, and selected event visibility.

Member-only

Salons and private notes

City access, issue deep dives, curated introductions, and member directory features.

Invite-only

Circles and retreats

Founder groups, family-office cohorts, spouse tracks, and destination experiences.

One of the strongest potential story avenues is to embed people at startups and companies, but also in their actual lives: homes, family systems, training routines, airports, retreats, dinners, and second-act projects.

Side Quests

What this could grow into if the brand becomes trusted.

The best side quests are not random. They are products that readers already want once they believe the brand has better taste, access, and filters than they do on their own.

01

Founder travel club

Curated itineraries, member trips, and destination experiences.

02

Ghostwriting and signal studio

Essays, talks, profiles, and reputation work for serious people.

03

Family constitution programs

Workshops, templates, and private sessions around wealth, values, and governance.
