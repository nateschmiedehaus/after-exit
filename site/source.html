Operating Plan

Company design, calendar, and delivery systems.

This page describes how After Exit is built and run: the planning unit, the core team, the quarterly calendar, the circulation and partner systems, and the measurement loops that govern the first year.

Operating Model

The issue is the planning unit for the whole company.

Editorial, membership, partner programs, and production all work off the same quarterly issue calendar. The venture is not run as separate content, events, and sponsorship businesses.

Editorial

Issue slate and archive

Theme selection, reporting, editing, visual direction, and archival continuity.

Membership

Rooms and reader routing

Applications, invitations, host management, member notes, and introductions.

Commercial

Partner programs

Category ownership, package design, renewals, delivery reporting, and benefits integration.

Production

Object and circulation chain

Design, print buying, fulfillment, mailing files, venue drops, and event operations.

Team and Ownership

Small core team, explicit owners, specialist bench.

The core team carries editorial judgment, audience ownership, commercial control, and calendar discipline. Specialist work expands and contracts by issue, city, and production load.

Lead 01

Editor in chief / publisher

Owns thesis, issue slate, subject access, final approvals, and cross-function decisions.

Lead 02

Managing editor / program operator

Owns the production calendar, contributor workflow, budget tracking, and issue close.

Lead 03

Membership and city lead

Owns charter intake, rooms, invitations, host relationships, and follow-through.

Lead 04

Founding partner lead

Owns partner pipeline, category discipline, package delivery, and renewals.

Specialist bench

Photo, film, design, copy editing, retouching, event labor.

These functions can be bought by issue or by city until cadence is established.

Operating rule

One owner per decision, one calendar for everyone.

The issue clock governs all promises made to readers, hosts, and partners.

Calendar System

Quarterly issue cycle, weekly editorial pulse, recurring room rhythm.

The quarterly issue is the anchor object. Weekly publishing keeps the brand active between issues. Rooms follow the issue calendar rather than operating as a separate social program.

Weeks 1-2

Commission

Weeks 3-6

Report

Weeks 7-9

Weeks 10-12

Weekly

Extend

Quarterly

One room tied directly to the new issue and its lead editorial package.

Monthly

City salons

Smaller hosted rooms for sourcing, member conversion, and partner access.

Selective

Closed circles

Small groups around allocation, governance, health, family, and second-act design.

Commercial and Circulation Operations

Membership, partners, and print run through one delivery chain.

Partner programs are tied to issue themes, room moments, and controlled circulation. Counts stay separated by channel from the first issue forward.

Reader operations include paid delivery, member copies, invitation handling, guest privileges, and post-room follow-up. Partner operations include issue placement, room presence, member benefits, target-account distribution, and delivery reporting. The print chain includes page planning, prepress, binding, mailing, venue drops, and circulation close.

Ad load stays low. Partner inventory stays narrow. The operating question is never “how many pages can be sold?” but “which categories belong in the object and how are those programs delivered cleanly?”

Measurement and Year One

The first year is judged by density, renewal, and repeatability.

The operating dashboard centers on who arrives, who stays, which rooms repeat, which partners renew, and whether the circulation chain holds together issue after issue.

Source quality

Tracked

Room yield

Repeat

Commercial health

Renew

Partner renewal, upsell into room programs, and category retention.
