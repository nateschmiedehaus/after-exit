Publication

The issue is the center of the venture.

After Exit is built around a quarterly magazine rather than around a feed, an event calendar, or a members-only forum. The issue is the thing the company publishes, circulates, discusses in rooms, and sells around.

Each issue combines one major reported feature, one embedded field story, portrait-driven profiles, practical notebooks, and a smaller back section for references, recommendations, and short intelligence. The reporting sits inside the places this audience actually lives: companies, homes, offices, training routines, travel schedules, and family systems.

The magazine behaves like a canonical object. It gives the category one shared frame, one visual language, and one thing that can be kept, handed over, cited, gifted, and placed in rooms where attention already has value.

Format and Circulation

The object moves through homes, offices, rooms, and partner contexts.

The magazine is both an editorial object and a circulation object. It reaches readers through direct delivery, host gifting, controlled placement, and event drops in the places where this audience already spends time.

Print changes the behavior of the product. The issue can sit on a desk, travel in a bag, appear in a living room, or be handed to another principal after a dinner. That gives the brand permanence and gives partner programs a context that email and audio do not create.

The circulation logic is selective rather than mass. Fewer copies in better hands creates more editorial signal and a more valuable advertiser environment than broad anonymous reach.

Commercial Design

Reader revenue and partner revenue are designed for scarcity.

Reader revenue starts with the issue and deepens into room access, member routing, and higher-trust circles. Partner revenue stays narrow: wealth, governance, health, travel, education, and other categories already embedded in the reader’s life.
